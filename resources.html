WA 211

This resource provides help for meals, emergency shelter/housing, medical/dental care, mental health, substance abuse, health insurance, transportation, support groups, social services, clothing, and more. It can be helpful for the elderly, disabled, those unable to speak english, those in crisis, etc. All you need to do is dial 211 in Washington State and a trained specialist will connect you to resources in your community.

Domestic Violence Services of Snohomish County

Contact the Domestic Violence Services of Snohomish County to get the help you need to save yourself or your loved ones. Your safety and privacy is their priority. You can call their free 24 hour support line to receive help from a trained advocate. DVS also provides a confidential temporary shelter for individuals and their children-including elderly people- who are going through intimate partner violence (IVP), or they can assist you in finding safe housing. Their legal advocates can help people through protection orders, court support, and connect them to legal services. DVS also offers free support groups every week, in Spanish and English. To spread awareness, they have free community education and training about Domestic Violence and Teen Dating Violence.

Edmonds Food Bank

The Edmonds Food Bank program helps feed women, men, children, and seniors in need. You can use their site to find the food bank closest to you for convenience. You can order food online through their webpage. Their home delivery option, though limited, can help those who are 60 and older or have transportation roadblocks in/around Edmonds. In addition, they partnered with several senior housing locations such as The Reserve, or Olympic View Apartments. They are also partners with different organizations to provide culturally appropriate food and language services, such as the Latino Educational Training Institute, or the Washington West African Center.
